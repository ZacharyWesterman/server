The tag query is a powerful option that lets you filter files by the state of their tags. There are a few operations you can use:
  • Tags: Anything in quotes is considered a tag. Non-quoted words are also tags, but those must not have any spaces or odd characters in them.
  • Operators: The operators and or or go inbetween tags, and filter files that either have both tags or one of them, respectively. You can also use &(and) or |(or).
  • Functions: These go before a tag and indicate that, instead of being a tag, it actually refers to the number of tags a file has. The available functions are eq(equals), lt(less than), le(less or equal), gt(greater than) and ge(greater or equal).
  • Parentheses: You can use parentheses to enforce a specific evaluation order. For example: "AB" or A and B is equivalent to ("AB" or A) and B, but you might want "AB" or (A and B).
